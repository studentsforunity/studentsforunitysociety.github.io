BASIS Meet Yourself 2022

On November 18 of 2022, Students For Unity along with many volunteers held a cultural showcase for 9 regions: India, Philippines, Vietnam, China, Middle East, Africa, Europe, and Latin America. The regions threw a performance and held tables selling regional food and showing cultural traditions. Thank you to Athena for being the head organizer for the event!

Philippines

Region Leader: Blessy

The Philippines table sold delicious Filipino snacks, including turon, halo-halo, and various chips/crackers along with offering teacher cardboard cutouts, dried mangoes, and chocolate for the raffles.

The Philippines table threw three performances:

1. Tinikling, a traditional folk dance involving two people dancing between two bamboo sticks being clicked. An amazing job to Kyla, Blessy, Mary Bless, William, Athena, and Therese!

2. Kahit Ayaw Mo Na, which translates to "Even if you don't want it anymore", is This Band's popular song encapsulating the emotions and hardships entailed in love. A beautiful cover done by Kiera!

3. Sarah Geronimo's hit song "Tala", translating to "star", is about the shining star within people. Thank you to Athena, Blessy, Kyla, and Kiera for ending the Filipino performances with a bang!

China

Region Leader: Daniel and Jennica

The China table threw three performances, all played live by musical instruments which highlighted the strong musical appreciation in ancient Chinese culture:

1. The first piece performed was titled “Butterfly Lovers (梁祝).” John Han performed on the viola, Daniel Wu performed on the violin, and Wenli Xu performed on the keyboard. This piece showcased a traditional Chinese music style with its light tone and melodic song structure.

2. The second piece was played by Nuoyan Zhang on the pipa (琵琶), entitled “Thunder in the Dry Season (旱天雷).” The pipa is a traditional Chinese instrument made up of fretted notes and played upright. The solo performance enveloped the event hall due to the pipa's unique sound and beautiful design.

3. The last piece was titled “Peacocks Flying Southeast (孔雀东南飞).” Rita Cai performed this piece on the guzheng (古筝), which is a traditional instrument originating from the Chinese Qin dynasty (221-206 BC). The resonant board of the instrument as well as the skillfully plucked strings created a striking performance that captivated the Meet Yourself event. Thank you to Rita Cai for coming and performing for this occasion!

The snacks at the China table included Jelly-based snacks, rice crackers, and mini cakes commonly consumed in the mainland country by children.
